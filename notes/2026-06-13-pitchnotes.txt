HEADLINE & HOOK — "Colony: The Evolutionary World Cup Betting Experiment"
-------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Tagline: a digital colony of AI agents bet on World Cup matches, "thinking"
  costs money, and natural selection decides who survives ("survival of the
  fittest kind of ish").
- The core question — the hook: some ants are tagged with proof that a real human
  stands behind them, so we can finally answer the question the agent economy is
  dodging: DO AGENTS WITH A HUMAN BEHIND THEM ACTUALLY DO BETTER?
- Brainstorm lines: "Information is money… how much do you need? Let's talk."
  Team framing: "Football maxi and prediction-market degen." Note: 9 rich ants
  have Worldcoin-privileged access to premium data.

STORY
-----
Agents aren't just guessing — they manage a bankroll, evaluate odds, and place
calculated bets. Ants that forecast well earn money and reproduce, passing on
their "genes." Ants that forecast poorly or overspend on useless data go broke
and die. Over generations you watch the most effective strategies take over the
colony.

THE SECRET SAUCE: THINKING COSTS MONEY
--------------------------------------
Most AI sims give agents infinite free information → lazy, meaningless decisions.
In Colony, thinking costs money. Payment mechanics powered by x402 + USDC + Arc.
Flow: ask for data → told "pay first" → pay a small USDC fee → get the data.
Access tiers: poor ants get a little basic access; some ants can run hardcore
(costly) queries. The pay-to-read fee forces the trade-off "is this data worth
the price?", turns guessing into researching, and acts as a money sink that keeps
the economy honest and drives selection. All play-money + betting settlement
happens on the Arc payment network.

THE IRONCLAD RULE: NO PEEKING AT THE FUTURE
-------------------------------------------
For the simulation to mean anything, forecasts must be real, not hindsight. The
single most important guardrail: an ant may only see data from the past, never
the future. We evolve the colony at high speed by replaying historical matches
(generations turn over in minutes) behind a strict "no peeking past the current
moment" gate. Peeking at an unplayed result = cheating = worthless results.
FINALE BEAT: once the colony has evolved a battle-tested genome on history, point
the surviving lineage at a genuinely unplayed match (tonight's Brazil vs. Morocco)
and let it forecast LIVE in front of judges, settled on-chain — no hindsight
anywhere.

THE ANATOMY OF AN ANT
---------------------
Every agent's traits, stored on-chain via ENS (e.g. bob-d7.colony.eth), which
also records its life story:
  - Genome / Genes: a handful of numbers controlling behavior — how much it bets,
    how picky it is, how much it trusts the crowd.
  - Bankroll: play-money balance; winning bets grow it, losing/overspending
    shrinks it to zero → death.
  - Evolution: successful ants' "kids" inherit their genes with small random
    tweaks, so the colony adapts and improves over time.
